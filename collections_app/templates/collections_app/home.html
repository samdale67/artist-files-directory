Why An Artist Files Directory?

If you are new to artist files, head to What Are Artist Files? for a primer.

Artist files are among the most valued and heavily used resources in art research facilities. These files often provide critical documentation about well established artists, as well as lesser known artists. Researchers rely on artist files to establish chronologies, flesh out exhibition histories, review stylistic developments, assess the critical reception of artists over time, among many other uses. Also, artist files collections frequently have regional strengths that make them particularly vital repositories for communities. Also, artist files sometimes contain unique objects, such as those highlighted in the New York Public Library's entry. This type of material, recognized by curators as helping to provide yet another angle on an artist, feature them in exhibitions.

❝ ... artist files [are] among the least accessible resources in research collections [and] the single most untapped resource to the broader art research community. ❞

Ironically, artist files continue to be among the least accessible resources in research collections. The very nature of the material, limited staff resources, and lack of technical or administrative support have all contributed to this problem ... As a result, it is only the most diligent of researchers that will discover these files by searching individual local catalogs or other publicly published indexes. In effect, this has made artist files the single most untapped resource to the broader art research community.

What is the Scope of the Directory?

While the first Artist Files Revealed publication and related (now defunct) online directory focused on institutional collections, the new directory expands coverage by offering access to personal collections of artist files. A number of new access options help researchers find collections by subjects, technical data such as special formats, physical location of the the collection, and even by collector categories. It also provides a dealer directory for sellers who specialize in artist ephemera.

Let's quickly get something out of the way: the directory is not a delivery platform for digitized or digital artist files content.
